# Amazon Product Reviews Sentiment Analysis

## Project Overview

This project performs sentiment analysis on Amazon product reviews using Natural Language Processing (NLP). Customer reviews are classified as Positive, Negative, or Neutral based on the emotional tone of the review text. The project also visualizes sentiment distribution and identifies the most common words used in customer feedback.

---

## Objectives

- Analyze customer reviews using NLP
- Classify reviews into Positive, Negative, and Neutral sentiments
- Explore customer opinions through visualizations
- Identify common themes in positive and negative reviews
- Generate business insights from customer feedback

---

## Dataset

- **Dataset:** Amazon Fine Food Reviews
- **Source:** Kaggle
- **File:** `Reviews.csv`

---

## Tools and Libraries

- Python
- Pandas
- Matplotlib
- TextBlob
- WordCloud

---

## Project Workflow

1. Load the dataset
2. Explore the dataset
3. Clean and prepare the data
4. Perform sentiment analysis using TextBlob
5. Visualize sentiment distribution
6. Compare ratings with predicted sentiment
7. Generate positive and negative word clouds
8. Interpret findings and summarize insights

---

## Visualizations Created

- Distribution of Customer Sentiments
- Average Rating by Sentiment
- Positive Review Word Cloud
- Negative Review Word Cloud

---

## Key Insights

- Most customer reviews express positive sentiment.
- Positive reviews are associated with higher customer ratings.
- Words such as **taste**, **great**, **love**, and **good** frequently appear in positive reviews.
- Negative reviews commonly include words such as **bad**, **disappointed**, **smell**, and **price**.
- Sentiment analysis provides valuable insights into customer opinions and product performance.

---

## Conclusion

This project demonstrates how Natural Language Processing can transform customer reviews into actionable insights. By combining sentiment classification with visualizations, businesses can better understand customer satisfaction, identify recurring issues, and make informed decisions to improve products and services.
